Module 6: Wireless Networking

Lesson

Introduction

Module 6 delves into the realm of Wireless Networking, a technology that has revolutionized the way we connect and communicate. Wireless networks provide flexibility and convenience, allowing users to access information and resources without being tethered to a physical connection.

Key Concepts in Wireless Networking

  • Wireless Standards: Understanding various wireless standards such as Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and Zigbee.
  • Wireless Security: Implementing security measures like WPA3 encryption, MAC address filtering, and disabling SSID broadcasting.
  • Wireless Access Points (WAPs): Configuring and managing WAPs to extend wireless coverage and improve network performance.
  • Wireless LANs and WANs: Differentiating between Local Area Networks (LANs) and Wide Area Networks (WANs) in wireless environments.
  • Wireless Troubleshooting: Identifying common issues and troubleshooting techniques in wireless networks.

Conclusion

In Module 6, we explored the intricacies of Wireless Networking, understanding its significance in modern connectivity. By grasping the underlying concepts and best practices, individuals and organizations can harness the power of wireless technology efficiently and securely.
